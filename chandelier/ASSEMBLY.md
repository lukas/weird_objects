# Polyhedron Constellation Chandelier — Minimalist Build Guide

> **This guide describes the recommended low-cost build path** ($900–$1,850
> all-in) using a fiber-optic light engine, stainless aircraft cable
> suspension, and 3D-printed plastic polyhedra around a small cast-metal
> star + canopy plate.
>
> For the *premium / fully-cast* alternative (every visible part in
> investment-cast A356 aluminum, $15k–$35k through Xometry), see
> [`ASSEMBLY_full_cast.md`](ASSEMBLY_full_cast.md).
>
> Both builds use the same 31-polyhedron geometry generated by
> `all_polyhedra.py` — they differ only in what is metal vs. plastic
> and in how the lighting is delivered.

---

## 1. The big idea

The full chandelier is a constellation of 31 polyhedra (5 Platonic + 13
Archimedean + 13 Catalan) arranged on three concentric rings, suspended
below a flat metal star at the top.  Two design choices collapse most of
the build complexity:

1. **One central LED fiber illuminator drives all 31 polyhedra in
   parallel.**  No internal LEDs, no per-polyhedron drivers, no wiring
   harness through the metal frame — just 31 polished end-glow PMMA
   fibers fanning out from a single light engine inside the canopy.
2. **Each polyhedron hangs from a stainless aircraft cable + fiber
   strand bundled together.**  The cable carries load; the fiber
   carries light; together they read as a single thin glowing thread
   from any normal viewing distance.

Result: only **three** parts are cast metal — the canopy plate, the
outer 13-point star, and the inner pentagram — and *everything else*
is FDM-printed plastic, laser-cut acrylic, or off-the-shelf cable and
fiber.

## 3. Components in the metal STL

Send `chandelier_metal_minimal.stl` to a casting house.  The STL
contains **3 separately-watertight cast bodies**:

| # | Body | Mass | Envelope | Function |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Canopy Plate + Eye | ~ 1.6 kg | 201 × 136 × 201 mm | Flat plate that bolts to the ceiling canopy; carries the eye on top, the fiber illuminator on its underside, and 31+ drilled holes for cable + fiber drops |
| 2 | Outer 13-Star | ~ 1.95 kg | 943 × 14 × 936 mm | Flat 13-pointed {13/5} star polygon at the top of the chandelier; suspends 26 of the 31 polyhedra (13 Archimedeans + 13 Catalans dangling below them) |
| 3 | Inner Pentagram | ~ 0.35 kg | 411 × 14 × 392 mm | Flat 5-pointed {5/2} pentagram, concentric with the 13-star; suspends the 5 Platonics |

**Geometry note:** the stars are flat castings.  All rod-on-rod
crossings inside each star are merged solid by the boolean union, so
each star is self-rigid as a single piece — no separate hub or spokes
needed to hold it together.

**Envelope note:** the outer 13-star is ~ 943 mm tip-to-tip, which is
above the working envelope of some investment-casting cells.  If the
foundry pushes back, the easiest fix is to ask them to **split it into
two halves at one of the central crossings** and rejoin with a stainless
sleeve + epoxy at install time.

## 5. Light path overview (read this first)

Before you start, here is the lighting routing end-to-end so the
sequence below makes sense:

```
              ┌──────── ceiling J-hook ────────┐
                          │
                    eye bolt + canopy
                  ┌───────┴────────┐
                  │ canopy plate    │
                  │ + LED illuminator (under-side)
                  │ + 31 fiber ports (one per polyhedron)
                  └─┬─┬─┬─┬─┬─┬─┬─┬┘
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │   ← 31 fibers + a few cables descend
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ ── 2 structural cables
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │     ↓ to outer 13-star and
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │     ↓ inner pentagram centres
                    │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
              ╔═════╧═╧═╧═╧═╧═╧═╧═╧══╗  ← outer 13-star + inner pentagram
              ║ flat metal star plane ║      (suspended at same Y)
              ╚═╤══╤══╤══╤══╤══╤══╤══╝
                │  │  │  │  │  │  │   ← 18 cable+fiber drops, one per star tip
                ▼  ▼  ▼  ▼  ▼  ▼  ▼
              [Plat] [Plat] ... [Arch] [Arch] ...   (5 Platonic, 13 Archimedean)
                                  │  │  │
                                  ▼  ▼  ▼
                                [Cat] [Cat] ...     (13 Catalan)
                                  │  │  │
                                  ▼  ▼  ▼   ← 13 cable+fiber drops, Arch → Cat
```

Each "cable+fiber drop" is one stainless aircraft cable (carries load)
twisted with one PMMA fiber strand (carries light), held together with
clear heat-shrink at ~ 100 mm intervals.  At the polyhedron, the fiber
exits the bundle and terminates at a frosted diffuser inside the cage;
the cable passes through a small grommet and terminates at the
polyhedron's top-vertex eye-bolt.

## 6. Manufacturing sequence

### 6.1 3D-print the 31 polyhedra

Generate per-polyhedron STLs with the bundled helper:

```bash
python split_polyhedra.py --out-dir polyhedra_stl
```

This writes 31 print-ready STLs into `polyhedra_stl/`, each:

- Sized to its post-cast diameter (~ 130 mm OD)
- Rotated so the top vertex points along +Z (printer "up")
- Translated so the lowest point sits at Z = 0 (build-plate datum)
- Pre-drilled with an **M3 heat-set insert pocket** (4.2 mm Ø × 5.5 mm
  deep) at the top vertex node so a brass insert can be melted in for
  the cable + eye-bolt attachment (see §7.3)

Pass `--skip-pocket` if you'd rather drill the hole yourself
post-print, or `--pocket-diameter` / `--pocket-depth` to match a
different brand of heat-set insert.

Print settings (PETG, 0.4 or 0.6 mm nozzle):

- Layer height **0.2–0.3 mm**
- 2–3 perimeters, **15 % gyroid infill** (interior is hollow anyway —
  infill only fills the rod and node walls)
- Print orientation: as exported (vertex-on-top, the same orientation
  it hangs in).  No supports needed — the wireframe geometry
  self-supports once the first ~ 10 mm of node sphere is past.
- The largest polyhedron envelope is ~ 140 × 140 × 140 mm (fits any
  standard ≥ 220 mm build plate).

Total print time at 0.3 mm layers, 15 % infill: ~ 60–90 hours total
across all 31 polyhedra on a single Bambu P1S / Prusa MK4.  Print
times per solid roughly track edge count — the Tetrahedron prints in
~ 1 hour, the Disdyakis Triacontahedron in ~ 6 hours.

### 6.2 Paint the polyhedra metallic

1. Hang each printed polyhedron from a string in a well-ventilated
   spray booth (or outdoors, no wind).
2. Light dust-coat with **automotive grey filler primer** — 1 pass, let
   it tack-dry 30 minutes.  This kills the print-line texture.
3. **Two passes** of metallic colour (champagne bronze recommended:
   Rust-Oleum Specialty Metallic in *Champagne* hits about the right
   warm-cast-aluminum tone), 10 minutes between passes.
4. Clear-coat: one light pass of Rust-Oleum Crystal Clear or any 2K
   automotive clear.  This is the difference between "looks like
   plastic up close" and "looks like brushed metal up close".

### 6.3 Install acrylic panels

Identical to the full-cast build — see
[`ASSEMBLY_full_cast.md` §5](ASSEMBLY_full_cast.md).  Slot the panels
into the printed slot grooves through each polyhedron's lowest face
(the open access face).

Tolerance note: FDM-printed slots are typically ±0.1 mm true to model,
which is *better* than cast slots.  No fitting issues expected.

### 6.4 Cast the metal subset

Send `chandelier_metal_minimal.stl` to your foundry of choice (§4.1
and §11).  When it arrives, drill these post-cast holes on a drill
press (use the printable jigs from §6.5 to make them repeatable):

1. **Drill the canopy plate** (4 holes total — the 31 fibers all
   exit the illuminator's bundle terminator together through the
   single central hole, then fan out below the canopy):
   - 1 × **15 mm** hole at the centre of the plate, for the
     fiber-optic bundle exiting the illuminator
   - 2 × **3 mm** holes at ±18 mm from centre, for the 2 structural
     cables that suspend the inner pentagram and outer 13-star
   - 1 × **9.5 mm** hole near the rim, for the wall-wart cord
     pass-through
2. **Drill 18 cable holes through the star tips.**  Each tip has a
   small node sphere; drill 1 × **2 mm** hole vertically through the
   centre of each tip node — 13 in the outer 13-star + 5 in the
   inner pentagram = 18 total.  These are the anchor points where
   the upper-ring (Platonic + Archimedean) cable+fiber drops attach.
3. **Drill 13 cable holes through the bottom-vertex of every
   Archimedean** (post-print, in plastic — no foundry involvement).
   Each Archimedean's bottom vertex node gets a 2 mm hole through
   it so the Catalan-suspending cable+fiber bundle can pass through
   from the Archimedean's interior to the Catalan dangling below.
4. **Bead-blast everything** if the foundry didn't.
5. **Clear-anodize or clear-coat** to protect the finish.

### 6.5 Print drill jigs

Two helper STLs make the post-cast drilling repeatable:

```bash
python make_drill_jigs.py --out-dir jigs
```

Produces:

- `jigs/canopy_drill_jig.stl` — slips over the canopy plate from
  above; through-holes in the jig top wall locate the 4 canopy
  holes (1 × 15 mm bundle, 2 × 3 mm structural cables, 1 × 9.5 mm
  cord)
- `jigs/star_tip_drill_jig.stl` — small cup that drops over a star
  tip node sphere from above with a hemispherical pocket for
  self-centering; through-hole guides the 2 mm bit perpendicular
  to the star plane through the node centre.  Print 2–4 copies so
  you can have a couple inked / marked while the rest are in use.

Print in PETG with 3 perimeters and 25 % infill (these jigs see
some load from the drill bit — don't go too thin).  Optional but
recommended: press in steel drill bushings (Slip-Renewable bushings
from McMaster — ~$4 each) for a long-life jig.

Drill pattern note: the canopy jig's hole positions are
**baked into the printed STL** at the locations specified in §6.4.
Don't try to override them at drilling time; if you need a
different pattern, edit `make_drill_jigs.py` and re-print.

## 7. Assembly sequence

### 7.1 Wire the cable + fiber drops

You will build **49 drops total**:

- 31 "polyhedron drops" — each one cable + one fiber strand
- 18 "star drops" (5 inner-pentagram → Platonic, 13 outer-13-star →
  Archimedean) are a *subset* of those 31, terminated at a star tip
  rather than the canopy plate
- 13 "Archimedean → Catalan drops" hang from the bottom of each
  Archimedean down to the top of its matched Catalan
- 2 "structural drops" — 2 mm cable only, no fiber, from canopy to
  star centres

For each polyhedron drop:

1. Cut a length of fiber: **1.5 m** for star-tip drops; **0.4 m** for
   Archimedean → Catalan drops.  Use sharp wire cutters; the cut end
   will be polished in step 4.
2. Cut a length of 0.8 mm cable to the same length minus 50 mm (cable
   terminates inside the polyhedron; fiber exits and continues to the
   diffuser).
3. Twist the two together, 1 turn per ~ 100 mm.  Slide a 50 mm length
   of clear 3 mm heat-shrink along, shrink in place.  Repeat at every
   100 mm to keep the bundle neat.
4. **Polish both fiber ends.**  Wet-sand on 400 grit until flat, then
   step through 600 / 1000 / 2000 grit.  An optical-quality end is
   visibly clear — if it looks frosted, keep sanding.  This step
   accounts for ~ 90 % of the lighting quality of the finished build,
   so don't skimp.
5. Crimp a ferrule on the cable end that will attach to the upstream
   anchor (canopy plate, star tip, or Archimedean bottom-eyelet).

### 7.2 Hang the structural cables

1. Suspend the **outer 13-star** and **inner pentagram** below the
   canopy plate using the two **structural** cables.  Loop each cable
   through the centre crossing of its star, run up through the
   matching 3 mm hole in the canopy plate, and crimp/ferrule on top.
2. The inner pentagram should hang at the same Y as the outer 13-star.
   Use the rest length of each star's centre crossing (i.e. the place
   where multiple rods overlap solid in the casting) as the
   attachment point.
3. Verify the star plane is **parallel to the floor** by eye or with a
   small bullseye level on the canopy plate; adjust the structural
   cables until level.

### 7.3 Hang the upper polyhedra (Platonics + Archimedeans)

For each of the 5 Platonics:

1. Take one of the 5 inner-pentagram drops.  Run the upper end of the
   cable through the appropriate **inner pentagram tip hole** and
   crimp.  Run the fiber through the corresponding **canopy plate
   inner-ring hole** and tie a small loose loop on top to keep it
   from falling out.
2. At the polyhedron's top vertex, thread the cable through the
   top-vertex hole and screw it into the M3 heat-set insert with an
   M3 eye-bolt.  Tighten until the polyhedron hangs vertical.
3. Hot-glue the fiber end into a frosted acrylic diffuser ball, drop
   the ball into the polyhedron through its open access face, and
   tuck it near the geometric centre.  The fiber should emerge from
   the top vertex hole inside the polyhedron and hang the diffuser
   ~ 30–50 mm above the access face.

Repeat for all 13 Archimedeans, using the outer 13-star tip holes.

### 7.4 Hang the Catalans

For each of the 13 Catalans:

1. The drop is already cable + fiber bundled.  The upper end attaches
   to the *bottom* vertex of the matched Archimedean (where the cable
   passes through the bottom-vertex 2 mm hole drilled in §6.4 step 3).
2. The fiber routes externally along the cable; it does **not** pass
   through the inside of the Archimedean.
3. Polyhedron-side: identical to step 7.3 step 2 — eye-bolt + diffuser
   ball.

### 7.5 Connect the fibers to the illuminator

1. Mount the LED illuminator on the underside of the canopy plate
   using two small screws or zip-ties through the cast plate's edge.
2. Trim each fiber's *upper* end to length so it just reaches the
   illuminator's bundle terminator.  Polish the tip (same 400/2000
   grit pass as in 7.1 step 4).
3. Insert each fiber into the illuminator's bundle terminator.  Most
   illuminators use a single ~ 30 mm-diameter port that all fibers
   share, held by a clamping nut.  Pack all 31 strands in evenly,
   tighten the nut.
4. Plug the wall-wart into the illuminator and into a spare AC outlet.
   The fibers should light up immediately; cycle through colours with
   the included IR remote to verify all 31 are seated.

### 7.6 Hang the chandelier

1. Locate a ceiling joist and install a **swag hook rated ≥ 50 lb**
   (most rated for 35 lb work but go up a size for safety margin —
   e.g. Hillman 853641, 50 lb capacity).
2. Slip the canopy plate's eye loop over the swag hook.
3. Power the wall-wart through a wall switch or smart plug.

## 9. Caveats & failure modes

1. **Single point of failure: the illuminator.**  If it dies, the
   whole chandelier goes dark.  Buy a spare ($80) and keep it in a
   drawer; replacement is plug-and-play (~ 5 minutes).
2. **PMMA fiber bend radius is ~ 10× diameter.**  A 1 mm fiber doesn't
   like bends tighter than 10 mm.  Plan cable routing so the fiber
   never sees a sharp turn — especially at the polyhedron entry
   point.  Chamfer those drilled holes before threading.
3. **Polish quality dominates output brightness.**  A chewed-up fiber
   end loses 50–80 % of its light.  Practice on scrap before doing
   the real strands.
4. **Cable terminations.**  Don't try to tie knots in stainless cable
   — it will kink and snap under load.  Always crimp ferrules.
5. **HVAC sway.**  The chandelier is now ~ 13 kg instead of the 17 kg
   of the full-cast version, so it'll respond more to drafts.  If
   that's bothersome, drop a 1 kg lead counterweight inside the
   canopy plate (zip-tie it to the underside next to the
   illuminator).
